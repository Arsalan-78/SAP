01 — Source Dossiers

Eight channels, evaluated individually

Ordered roughly by free-tier generosity. Each card lists the open-source tool(s) actually maintained as of mid-2026, what data comes back, and the daily ceiling before a source starts pushing back with CAPTCHAs, rate limits, or account flags.

SOURCE 01 / LOCAL BUSINESS DIRECTORY

Google Maps

Low risk

The strongest free option on this list by a wide margin. omkarcloud/google-maps-scraper is the most-starred tool in this category (2.8K+ GitHub stars) and ships a free tier of 200 searches per month, with each search returning 100–1,000+ results — enough to clear 10,000 leads a month without spending anything. gosom/google-maps-scraper is the better pick if you want to self-host on your own hardware indefinitely, since it has no built-in monthly cap and supports concurrent browser pooling for higher throughput.

The catch is email coverage: only roughly 30% of Maps listings link to a website at all, and open-source scrapers generally don't crawl into that website to extract an email — that's a separate enrichment step you'd need to build or do manually.

  • omkarcloud/google-maps-scraper200 searches/mo free, Botasaurus-based
  • gosom/google-maps-scraperGo, self-hosted, no monthly cap
  • Zubdata/Google-Maps-ScraperPython GUI, GPL license
Data returnedName, address, phone, website, rating, review count, lat/long, category
Daily ceiling (free)300–500 leads/day
Hard limit120 results per single search query
Email captureNot included — needs separate website crawl
Block behaviorCAPTCHA after sustained high-volume requests

SOURCE 02 / B2B SERVICE-PROVIDER DIRECTORY

Clutch.co

Medium risk

Clutch is purpose-built for exactly this use case — it's a directory of agencies and B2B service companies with structured profile data (rates, employee count, location, founding year, services). There's no official free API, so every option here is an unofficial scraper. gurza/clutch.co-scraper is a clean Scrapy-based implementation that pulls company name, URL, hourly rate, and tech focus into CSV. epctex/clutchco-scraper (built for the Apify platform) is faster — roughly 100 listings every 2 minutes — and returns the deepest profile data, including verification status and client-focus breakdowns.

None of these are officially sanctioned, so Clutch's own anti-bot layer is the limiting factor, not a published rate limit. Spreading requests over the day rather than bursting is what keeps this in the medium-risk tier rather than high.

  • gurza/clutch.co-scraperScrapy, free, CSV/JSON output
  • epctex/clutchco-scraperApify actor, ~100 listings/2 min
  • prikid/clutch.co_scraperLightweight, JSON-lines output
Data returnedCompany name, site, hourly rate, employees, location, services, rating
Daily ceiling (free)100–200 companies/day
Email captureRare — profiles don't usually expose direct email
Block behaviorIP-level throttling on sustained crawl bursts
Best fitAgency/vendor leads, partnership prospecting

SOURCE 03 / LEGACY LOCAL DIRECTORY

Yellow Pages

Medium-high risk

Yellow Pages remains a workable fallback when Google Maps coverage runs thin for a category, since it indexes a long tail of small local businesses that never built a Maps listing. Coverage is good for traditional trades (contractors, repair shops, local services) but weaker for anything digital-native. There is no single dominant open-source repo here the way there is for Maps — most teams write a thin Scrapy or BeautifulSoup parser against the public search results pages, since the HTML structure is comparatively simple and has stayed stable for years.

The trade-off is data quality: phone and address are reliable, but email and website fields are frequently blank or outdated, since many listings are years old and unmaintained by the business owner.

  • Custom Scrapy/BS4 parsersNo dominant maintained repo — DIY pattern
  • Apify Yellow Pages actorsFree-tier compute available
Data returnedName, address, phone, category, sometimes website
Daily ceiling (free)100–200/day
Email captureRare

Best fitTraditional trades, brick-and-mortar long tail
Reliability signal
SOURCE 04 / LOCAL BUSINESS DIRECTORY (SECONDARY)

Bing Maps

Medium-high risk

Bing Maps is best treated as a deduplication and cross-check layer rather than a primary source — its business listing data overlaps heavily with Google Maps but is sourced and refreshed independently, so it occasionally surfaces listings Google has delisted or not yet indexed. Microsoft does publish an official Bing Maps API, but the free tier is transaction-capped and oriented around mapping/geocoding use cases, not bulk business-data extraction, so most lead-gen workflows still go through unofficial scraping of the public search results instead.

Treat this as a secondary pass after Google Maps and Yellow Pages, run against categories or geographies where the primary two sources came back thin.

  • Bing Maps REST API (official)Free tier, transaction-capped, geocoding-focused
  • Unofficial Bing local-search scrapersVarious small repos, low maintenance activity
Data returnedName, address, phone, category, rating
Daily ceiling (free)100–200/day via unofficial route
Official APIFree tier exists, capped transaction volume
Block behaviorStandard CAPTCHA/IP throttling on the web UI
Best fitGap-filling, cross-verification of Maps data
Reliability signal
SOURCE 05 / LOCAL + SERVICE BUSINESS REVIEWS

Yelp

Medium risk

Yelp's strength is review-volume and category density for service businesses — restaurants, contractors, local professional services — making it useful for sales teams targeting that segment specifically. jamesbwang/yelp-scraper and TechWithTy/ScrapyYelpScrapper are both Scrapy-based and actively maintained, returning address, phone, website, and category in structured form. Yelp does also offer an official Fusion API, but its free tier is heavily restricted to display use cases (showing Yelp data back to consumers) rather than bulk lead export, so it's not a practical substitute here.

Data quality is generally good for the categories Yelp covers well, but coverage drops off fast for B2B/SaaS-type businesses that don't fit a consumer review model.

  • jamesbwang/yelp-scraperScrapy + pandas, sector + page-depth params
  • TechWithTy/ScrapyYelpScrapperScrapy, Streamlit UI, MIT license
  • abdalrhman-abas-0/yelp_scraperResumable crawl with interruption handling
Data returnedName, address, phone, website, category, review count
Daily ceiling (free)150–300/day
Official APIFree tier exists, but display-only — not built for export

SOURCE 06 / FUNDING + COMPANY INTELLIGENCE

Crunchbase

Medium risk

The best free source on this list for B2B/SaaS targeting specifically — Crunchbase exposes funding stage, investor names, and leadership profiles, which is exactly the qualifying data a sales team needs to prioritize outbound. scrapefulldotcom/crunchbase-scraper is built to work without a Crunchbase account at all, pulling company name, URL, rank, region, and a short description from public profile pages. FredericoBaker/crunchbase-scraper goes deeper into funding-round history but requires a Crunchbase Premium account (paid) to reach that data, so it's only listed here for awareness, not as a free-tier option.

Crunchbase's own free web tier already rate-limits page views per session before prompting a signup wall, so realistic free daily volume is lower here than on Maps or Clutch.

SOURCE 07 / DECISION-MAKER + COMPANY DATA

LinkedIn

High risk

The richest data on this list — names, titles, companies, employee counts — and the riskiest to pull for free. LinkedIn's Terms of Service explicitly prohibit automated scraping, and its detection systems actively look for it. The 2019–2022 hiQ Labs v. LinkedIn ruling established that scraping public data isn't a CFAA crime, but that's a legal floor, not protection from LinkedIn restricting or permanently banning the account doing the scraping — which is the practical risk that matters for a sales team. Several actively-maintained open-source options exist (joeyism/linkedin_scraper, Playwright-based; eracle/OpenOutreach, which adds AI-driven lead qualification on top), but every credible source on this is consistent: realistic safe volume on a free, account-based scraper is under 50 profiles/day on a single account before ban risk climbs sharply.

Recommendation: do not run this against your team's primary LinkedIn accounts. If this source is wanted at all, it needs a dedicated throwaway account, very conservative pacing, and sign-off that the team accepts the account-loss risk.

  • joeyism/linkedin_scraperPlaywright, async, requires logged-in session
  • eracle/OpenOutreachAI-qualified discovery + email fallback
  • linkdAPI/linkedin-leads-discoverFree-trial credits, no LinkedIn login needed
Data returnedName, title, company, location, headline, experience

SOURCE 08 / COMMUNITY / INTENT SIGNALS

Reddit

High risk — effectively closed for sales use

Reddit isn't a lead directory the way the other seven sources are — it never returns business names, addresses, or contacts directly. Its only realistic use case is intent monitoring: watching subreddits for people describing a problem your product solves, then reaching out manually. Even that narrower use case got materially harder in May 2026, when Reddit deprecated unauthenticated .json endpoint access, which had been the backbone of free, low-volume Reddit data pulls for years. What's left is the official OAuth API via PRAW, free at 60 requests/minute — but Reddit's terms are explicit that this free tier is non-commercial only. Commercial use, including sales monitoring, requires a paid agreement (commercial tier pricing runs around $0.24 per 1,000 calls, and isn't transparently published).

Using the free PRAW tier for a sales pipeline is a direct ToS violation, not a gray area like the other sources on this list. The realistic path here is either paying for commercial API access, or dropping Reddit from the board entirely and relying on the other seven sources.

03 — Suggested Rollout

A practical order to build this in

Matches the structure already used for the social-media automation board — start with the safest, highest-volume sources, automate those first, and treat the risky ones as a separate, explicitly-approved track.

01

Build first — low risk, high volume

These can run on a schedule (e.g. daily n8n cron job) without much oversight.

  • Google Maps — primary volume source
  • Yelp — fills service-business gaps
  • Clutch.co — B2B/agency targeting
02

Build second — useful, needs pacing

Worth automating, but with built-in rate limits and weekly (not hourly) refresh cycles.

  • Crunchbase — funding-stage qualification
  • Yellow Pages — long-tail local coverage
  • Bing Maps — cross-check / gap-fill pass
03

Hold for sign-off — real exposure

Don't automate until someone with authority accepts the account/legal risk in writing.

  • LinkedIn — throwaway account, manual pacing only
